# Ad creative brief — first 90 days

This document is the blueprint for the videos, images, and copy you'll feed into Facebook/Instagram, Google, and (optionally) LinkedIn and TikTok.

**Read first:** every claim in every ad must be **true at the moment you publish**. Australian Consumer Law section 18 (misleading conduct) and section 29 (false representations about services) apply to paid ads exactly the same as to your website. Reuse the "What you can and cannot say" rules from your strategy doc, section 7.

## The core proposition

We're a two-sided marketplace **and** a head contractor. The ad story has three beats:

1. **Insight** — Cleaning is broken in B2B. Inconsistent quality, opaque pricing, missed cleans, hard to switch.
2. **Mechanism** — We match the right cleaner to the right premises. We do the sourcing, vetting, contracts and admin. You get one accountable contact.
3. **Proof** — Real local operator, $10M insurance, written agreements, fixed pricing, above-award rates for subs.

Every ad picks ONE beat and goes deep. Don't try to say everything in 15 seconds.

**Hook:**
> "Quick maths: the Cleaning Services Award minimum for a Level 2 commercial cleaner is $X/hr. Here's what we pay..."

## Production constraints (read before recording)

- **Phone camera is fine.** Real and slightly rough out-performs slick and corporate for local services. Don't over-produce.
- **Vertical 9:16 for short-form** (Reels, TikTok, Stories), square 1:1 for Meta feed, horizontal 16:9 for YouTube and LinkedIn.
- **Captions burned in.** 80% of Meta/TikTok views are muted. Captions are not optional.
- **Your face on camera.** Stock footage of "cleaners" looks like every other ad. Your face — same one as the website's About page — is the trust signal.
- **One claim per ad.** Don't try to fit everything. If a claim doesn't have proof on the landing page, cut it.
- **Real footage where possible:**
  - You walking through a site (with permission)
  - Your van / kit
  - A real "before and after" of an actual job (with permission)
  - Avoid generic stock images of cleaners in fake settings

## Targeting setup

### Meta (Facebook / Instagram)

**Business audience**
- Location: Melbourne, 15km radius around target suburbs (Northern + Inner)
- Age: 28–60
- Job titles: Facilities Manager, Office Manager, Practice Manager, Operations Manager, Centre Manager, General Manager
- Interests: Commercial real estate, small business, body corporate, business networking (BNI)
- **Exclude:** People who've engaged with /contractor in last 30 days

**Contractor audience**
- Location: Melbourne, 25km radius around your target subcontractor suburbs
- Age: 22–55
- Languages: English + Hindi, Nepali, Punjabi, Vietnamese, Spanish (large Melbourne cleaning workforces)
- Interests: Cleaning services, Gumtree, Indeed, small business ownership, ABN
- **Exclude:** People who've engaged with /business in last 30 days

**Budget for first 14 days:** $15/day per audience = $30/day total. Run all 6 ad variations in one big ad set with audience-level optimisation. After 14 days, pause anything with CPL > $50 and double budget on whatever's working.

### Google Ads

Two campaigns: **Search** (high-intent) and **Performance Max** (broad).

**Search campaign — keywords to bid on**
| Keyword | Match type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| commercial cleaning melbourne | phrase | Bid hard — high intent |
| office cleaning {{suburb}} | phrase | One ad group per suburb (Thomastown, Reservoir, Preston, etc) |
| medical cleaning melbourne | phrase | High value, low competition |
| body corporate cleaning melbourne | phrase | Niche, less competition |
| childcare cleaning melbourne | phrase | Compliance buyer, less price-sensitive |
| commercial cleaners hiring melbourne | phrase | **Contractor audience** — point to /contractor |
| cleaning subcontractor melbourne | phrase | Contractor audience |
| {{competitor name}} review | phrase | Stealthy but allowed |
| cleaning quote melbourne | phrase | Could be residential — set negative keywords carefully |

**Negative keywords (set in account):**
- "carpet cleaning" (different vertical, residential bleed)
- "free", "cheap", "diy"
- "jobs" (unless you're targeting the contractor ad group)
- "tafe", "course", "training" (people studying cleaning, not buying)

**Budget:** $30/day for Search, $20/day for Performance Max. Performance Max will burn your budget faster but discovers audiences you wouldn't have targeted.

### LinkedIn (optional — only if you have time)

LinkedIn is the most expensive per-click but converts B2B at the highest rate.

- Target: "Facilities Manager", "Office Manager", "Practice Manager" + location Melbourne
- Format: Single-image with a one-line credibility hook ("Above-award rates for cleaners, fixed-price agreements for businesses") or a sponsored InMail to specific named decision-makers.
- Budget: $20/day. LinkedIn CPL will be $80–$200 — only run it if your contract values support that.

### TikTok (optional — only for contractor audience)

The Melbourne cleaning workforce is on TikTok (often more than Facebook). Run only Concept D/F (contractor concepts) at first.
- Format: 9:16 vertical, max 30 seconds, captions on.
- Budget: $10/day. Conversion tracking via the TikTok pixel (already wired in your `AdPixels` component if you set `NEXT_PUBLIC_TIKTOK_PIXEL_ID`).

## Tracking — what to set up before running a dollar

In Cloudflare Pages → Settings → Environment variables, set:

```
NEXT_PUBLIC_META_PIXEL_ID          = 1234567890123456
NEXT_PUBLIC_GOOGLE_ADS_ID          = AW-1234567890
NEXT_PUBLIC_GOOGLE_ADS_LEAD_LABEL  = (from Google Ads → Tools → Conversions → New "Lead" → Get tag)
NEXT_PUBLIC_LINKEDIN_PARTNER_ID    = 1234567 (optional)
NEXT_PUBLIC_TIKTOK_PIXEL_ID        = CXXXXXXX... (optional)
NEXT_PUBLIC_FUNNEL_VIDEO_URL       = https://youtu.be/xxxxxxxxxxx (set this when your explainer is uploaded)
```

After re-deploying, every form submission on /business, /contractor, and the residential /quote will fire:
- Meta Pixel `Lead` event with `content_category` set to the form type
- Google Ads conversion event using your "Lead" label
- GA4 `generate_lead` event
- LinkedIn `Conversion` event (if conversion ID set)
- TikTok `SubmitForm` event

You'll see the leads attribute back to which platform / campaign drove them — which is the only way to know what's working.

## Weekly review ritual (Mondays, 30 minutes)

| Step | What to look at |
|---|---|
| 1. Spend per platform | $/day for the last 7 days. Anything wildly over budget? |
| 2. Cost per lead per platform | Meta vs Google vs LinkedIn vs TikTok |
| 3. Lead quality | Pull the leads table from D1 — how many of last week's leads were genuine? |
| 4. Conversion to quote sent | What % of leads got a real fixed-price proposal back from you? |
| 5. Pause losers | Anything with CPL > $80 and no quotes sent after 30 days — pause. |
| 6. Double winners | Anything with CPL < $40 and at least one quote sent — double the budget. |

## What to do BEFORE you press "publish" on the first ad

1. Have at least **2 case studies** or **3 Google reviews** on the website. Cold paid traffic with no social proof = wasted spend.
2. Have your **phone on you and a fast email reply habit**. The single biggest determinant of paid-ads conversion is reply speed. Target: reply within 30 minutes during work hours, within 2 hours after.
3. Have the **capability statement PDF** ready to attach to email replies. (Template in `ops/outreach/capability-statement-template.md`.)
4. Have a **simple "Thanks for your enquiry" auto-reply** set up via Cloudflare Email Routing or your form handler. Sets expectation while you reply manually.
5. Have a **calendar booking link** (Cal.com free tier, or Google Calendar's Appointment Schedule). When someone replies, send the link — don't play email-tag.
