# Outbound email sequence — businesses

Every template is **written to be personalised**. The first sentence is the personalisation slot. Without that sentence, the email is mail-merge and goes in trash.

Use these as starting points. Edit per recipient before sending. **Never** send unedited.

## Subject lines that get opens

Pick one per email. Avoid "Quick question" and "Following up" — they trigger spam filters. Avoid emojis on B2B cold mail.

- `Cleaning for {business name}`
- `Quick note about {suburb} commercial cleaning`
- `{premises type} cleaning — fixed pricing`
- `For {decision-maker first name} — cleaning options`
- `Re: {recent google review snippet}`  *(if responding to a public complaint)*
- `When is your cleaning contract up for review?`

## Personalisation slot — examples

Spend 30 seconds finding a real, specific opener. Templates are nothing without it.

**For medical/childcare:**
- "I saw your team is expanding — congrats on the second consulting room."
- "Noticed Sarah just joined as practice manager — passing my note via her."

**For office:**
- "Walked past your office on Tuesday afternoon — the foyer plants are looking sharp."
- "Saw the LinkedIn post about hitting 30 staff — congrats."

**For retail:**
- "Was in the shop on Saturday — picked up the new range."

**For body corporate:**
- "Saw the recent OC notice about the carpark — happy to include carpark sweeping in any cleaning scope."

**For tenders / formal procurement:**
- "Saw the tender notice on Buying for Victoria for {{tender_ref}}."
- (Skip the chat — go straight to capability and pricing.)

## Calling script — Day 8 phone touch

Cold-call reception, never the decision-maker. Goal: get the decision-maker's direct email or a callback time. 30 seconds, friendly, low-pressure.

> "Hi, this is {{your_name}} from CS Cleaners. I sent {{first_name}} an email about commercial cleaning earlier this week — wondering if you could let me know if they got it, or pass on a good time to reach them? Happy to wait until they're ready."

If asked what it's about:
> "Just a fixed-price quote on commercial cleaning, no obligation. Takes 10 minutes."

If they offer to take a message:
> "Thanks — just my name, {{your_name}}, CS Cleaners, 0404 378 911. Tell them no rush."

---

## What NOT to do

- Mass merge without per-recipient edits. Facilities managers smell this in one second.
- Open with "I hope this email finds you well." It signals template.
- Attach a 4-page brochure. The 1-page capability statement is enough.
- Send more than 4 cold emails before phone. After 4 you're spam.
- Send any email after 6pm. They look automated.
- Use "guaranteed" without something specific to guarantee.
- Promise savings percentages without a quote in hand. ACL section 29.
